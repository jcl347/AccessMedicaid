Help in your language

Pick your language for the basics below, read the whole site in your language with the Language button at the top, and remember: a free interpreter is your right.

Every Medi-Cal plan must give you a free interpreter - by phone or in person - in any language, including American Sign Language. You never have to bring your own, and a child should never have to translate for you.

How to ask: when you call any number on this page, say your language (for example, "Español," "中文," "Tiếng Việt," "한국어," "Հայերեն," "Tagalog," "العربية"). They will connect a free interpreter.

Read everything in your language: use the Language button at the top of the page; it translates the whole site.

Don't lose your coverage

How to renew your Medi-Cal

You must renew once a year to keep your coverage. Most people who lose Medi-Cal still qualified - they just missed the paperwork. Here is how to stay covered.

  1. Keep your contact info current. Make sure your county has your right address, phone, and email so you get your renewal packet. Moved recently? Update it now.
  2. Watch your mail. Your county mails a renewal packet (often a yellow envelope) once a year. Open it right away and note the due date.
  3. Renew the easy way. Online at BenefitsCal.com is fastest. You can also renew by phone with your county, by mail, or in person. Some people are renewed automatically and just need to confirm.
  4. Send any documents on time. If they ask for proof (income, address), send it by the due date. If you miss it, you still have 90 days after coverage ends to turn in your paperwork and get it back.
  5. Get free help if you are stuck. Call your county, your health plan's Member Services, or the free Health Consumer Alliance. Interpreters are free.
Renew at BenefitsCal.com LA County DPSS: 1-866-613-3777 Health Care Options: 1-800-430-4263 Free help: 1-888-804-3536 Set a yearly reminder

Outside Los Angeles County? Renew through your own county office. Your county's number is on your renewal packet, or call Health Care Options above.

Here are easy ways to find out:

  • Look at your Medi-Cal member ID card - your plan name and Member Services number are printed on it.

  • Call Health Care Options at 1‑800‑430‑4263 (free) to ask which plan you're in or to change plans.

Get ready for your visit

A little prep helps you get more from every appointment. Print this and bring it with you.

Questions to ask your doctor
  • What is my main health problem, in plain words?
  • What are my choices for treatment? What are the benefits and risks?
  • Will this cost me anything? (Most Medi-Cal care is free.)
  • What should I do at home, and when should I call you?
  • Do I need any tests, referrals, or a follow-up visit?
  • Can I get my prescriptions for less or by mail?
  • Can we do the next visit by phone or video (telehealth)?
Ask for a free interpreter

You have the right to a free interpreter at every visit. You should never have to bring your own or use a child to translate. When you book and when you arrive, say:

"I need a [your language] interpreter for my appointment, please. It is my right under Medi-Cal and it is free."

Examples: Spanish - "Necesito un interprete de espanol, por favor." · 中文 - "我需要中文翻译。" · Tieng Viet - "Toi can thong dich vien tieng Viet."

What to bring
  • Your member ID card and your Medi-Cal BIC card
  • A photo ID
  • A list of your medicines (or the bottles)
  • Your questions (above) and a pen
  • A list of your symptoms and when they started
  • Any past records or test results you have

About this guide

Access Medi-Cal LA is a free navigation guide for Medi-Cal (Medicaid) members across the greater Los Angeles region. It gathers the phone numbers and links you need into one calm, simple place.

⚠️ Please double-check before you rely on a number. This guide is not the government and is not your health plan. Plans, phone numbers, and benefits can change. Always confirm important details with your plan's Member Services line or your county office. In a medical emergency, call 911.
